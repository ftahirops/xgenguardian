## XGenGuardian Launches Public Beta of Protective DNS That Catches Zero-Day Phishing Before Blocklists See It

> Visual brand-impersonation engine plus Certificate Transparency monitoring identifies phishing sites within minutes of registration — hours faster than Google Safe Browsing and VirusTotal.

**[CITY, DATE]** — XGenGuardian today launched the public beta of its brain-first protective DNS service, designed to catch zero-day phishing sites that existing blocklist-based DNS providers miss. The product is available immediately at xgenguardian.com, with a free tier and a paid Plus tier at $2.99 per month.

Unlike traditional protective DNS providers such as Quad9, NextDNS, and Cloudflare 1.1.1.2 — which rely on aggregated threat-intelligence feeds updated hours after a phishing kit goes live — XGenGuardian subscribes to the Certificate Transparency log firehose in real time. When a newly issued TLS certificate matches a brand-lookalike pattern, the system sandbox-renders the candidate page and compares its visual identity to a registry of known legitimate login pages using a CLIP image embedding. If the page looks like Brand X but isn't hosted on Brand X's infrastructure, it's blocked — typically before the first user clicks the link.

"Modern phishing kits live four to six hours," said [founder name], founder of XGenGuardian. "By the time PhishTank or Google Safe Browsing updates a blocklist, the victims have already been phished. Our system doesn't wait for someone else to label the site — it derives the verdict from the page's own visual identity."

Every block produced by XGenGuardian includes a public evidence page: the sandboxed screenshot, the visual-similarity score against the impersonated brand, the domain's registration age, the host ASN, the form-action target where submitted credentials would be exfiltrated, and a plain-English explanation generated by a language model. The transparency portal is publicly accessible at report.xgenguardian.com and accepts any URL for ad-hoc analysis.

The service runs as a DoH/DoT endpoint — users change one DNS setting on their device, router, or browser to enable protection. There is no client install, no root certificate authority to trust, and no TLS interception. An optional browser extension provides per-page evidence and tracker blocking but does not intercept HTTPS.

XGenGuardian's free tier includes the threat-intelligence blocklist, a newly-registered-domain filter, and a per-user dashboard. The Plus tier ($2.99/month) adds the visual brand-impersonation engine and behavioral sandbox. A Family tier ($5.99/month for six profiles) and a Business tier ($8/user/month with SSO and SIEM integration) are also available.

The architecture and full implementation plan are published at xgenguardian.com/architecture. A Phase-1 reference implementation of the resolver and analysis pipeline is open-sourced under MIT at github.com/xgenguardian/xgenguardian.
